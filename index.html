Philosophy of Prayer

Attitude of prayer
Simone Weil mentions that prayer requires attention or focus. I agree with the idea that prayer requires a focus not because God won’t hear us but that we might not hear God if we don’t focus on what we are saying and feeling. Prayer for me is most effective when I’m alone and in a quiet place that is free from distractions. I try and treat it as a conversation, and I know that majority of the time I’ll have to do a lot of the talking. I share what is going on as if God knew nothing about my life. It helps me to freely discuss what is going on and what my worries are. I try to treat the prayer as if it was between me and a parent or someone who loves me. I find these prayers leave me feeling more peaceful.
What prayer can do
Prayer can lead to miracles. I know no other way of putting it. Those miracles can be diverse things including healing, blessings, help, and change. In the movie Shadowlands, C.S Lewis mentions that prayer might not change anything that is going on in our lives but through prayer, God can change us. On my mission, I had a rough time a couple of weeks into Covid. Nothing was going the way I hoped it would. I started to pray while writing in my journal about what was going on in my life. I expressed how angry, frustrated, and helpless I felt. At the end of that prayer, I had received some knowledge that I wasn’t expecting. God didn’t justify how I felt instead he showed me things that I could not see at the time. Nothing changed about the situation I was in, but I made it past day 24 of being confined to an apartment and made it to the 9-month mark. Nothing had astronomically changed about my situation it still sucked but I was changed which made something more possible.
Why I pray
C.S Lewis shares “He gave us small creatures the dignity of being able to contribute to the course of events in two different ways”. He explains that one of these ways is through prayer. I’ve seen this demonstrated when I’ve knelt on my knees during difficult moments and prayed for relief and been given the capacity to be cleaned of the worries of what I was struggling through. I pray because I know that I can learn about God. He teaches me as I communicate with him showing me things that I never would have been able to acknowledge without him. The main reason I pray might be more towards the pragmatist side. I pray because I’ve experienced it working in my own life and so I continue to do so.

Acknowledging Difficulties

In a Beautiful Day in the Neighborhood, Mr. Rogers explains that talking about something makes it a human problem. When something is a human problem, it becomes manageable. In my relationships, I’ve seen problems arise because something couldn’t be discussed. Someone was hurting and neither of us could help the other because we didn’t know how to talk about it and when we tried, we never managed to get to the heart of what was going on. I’ve found that I need to discuss challenging things when they arise. I feel morally obligated to do so because I’ve seen how much different life can be when we struggle through things together instead of being alone. In the Shadowlands, Joy discusses with C.S Lewis that her death they need to talk about. She explains that it will help both to enjoy what is going on know if they acknowledge the suffering that is to come. The scriptures teach us that we need to mourn with those that mourn, and I think this principle starts with being able to talk about difficult topics.

Truth

Imbalances in Truth
The Apology by Plato describes a cave in which a group is trapped and living in a small world oblivious to the opportunities around them. One moral of the story is that not everyone wants to change and not everyone has access to the truth that is out there. I believe that the imbalances of truth that occur shouldn’t be fixed against the will of those around us. We can’t force anyone to leave the cave. We need to live in a way where others might want to change and seek the information for themselves. We can even invite people to listen to what we have to say but we can’t force the information on those around us. In Theaetetus by Plato, a story is told of Socrates’ quest to enlighten everyone around him that they don’t know what they think they know. I don’t believe in this story Socrates went about his goal in the right way. In the end, he was sentenced to death because he was disturbing people and refusing to back down. His goal might have been good but how he was trying to accomplish it led him to be incapable of continuing.
Sources of Truth
Chauncey C. Riddle in his essay Ways of Knowing discusses his thoughts on sources of knowledge. At the end of his essay, he writes about personal revelation. He says, “A wise Latter-day Saint will know and use all of the epistemologies, employing each where most needed and most valuable”. I agree with Chauncey in the fact that we need to not rely only on one source of knowledge. God has given the world so many ways to learn and grow. Each epistemology can help someone learn things that he wouldn’t have been able to learn using another epistemology. There are even some people who have preferences in their ways of learning. My favorite epistemology is probably technology as it requires me to learn how things work and why they work the way they do.
Relative Truth
In the autobiography of Gandhi, there’s an idea discussed relative truth. He says, “as long as I have not realized this Absolute Truth, so long must I hold by the relative truth as I have conceived it”. He describes that he can only see glimpses of the Absolute Truth and since he can’t see the full picture, he must rely on what he does know or what he does understand. This is very important in my life because it helps me to admit that I don’t know everything, but I can move forward with the things that I do know. Life is all about seeking for and finding truth and I know that we will only get a little here and a little there. The bible teaches that we will learn here a little there a little grace by grace. This should also help me to be humble because I know that there is more for me to learn. If I want to receive more, then admitting that there is more to receive is one of the first steps.

Ethics of Change

Gandhi the movie showed how Gandhi went about making change happen in South Africa and India. He focused on specific attainable goals and showed his support for people who were oppressed. I think this was a great way of going about change. Gandhi was willing to accept the consequences of breaking laws and wasn’t trying to avoid the punishments that the law would give. He didn’t create hate, but he would not tolerate hate from others. He fought their hate in peaceful ways. If change is to be brought about it should be done in ways that show how much you care about the people. It needs to be done in ways that fight the specific problem that you see but you also must accept the consequences of your actions. Gandhi made the decisions he did to establish change because the goal wasn’t only to change the government but to change the people themselves. His goal was to create a society that could follow through with the changes he sought in government. Change means little if that change isn’t followed through and defended.

Self-Care
Marcus Aurelius expresses the idea that all men are linked together and that to act against another person is contrary to nature. I believe that being a good neighbor entails looking out for one another and treating each other with mutual respect. I know that when I’m upset with someone that generally hurts my relationship with myself. Aurelius also mentions the idea that Good and bad things happen to everyone. This helps me to see that we shouldn’t judge people on what they are going through. We shouldn’t leave someone behind just because they live a different life than us. We shouldn’t let the past of those we spend time with stop us from being their friend. We do need to worry about how they act currently, but we need to allow people the opportunity to change. Aurelius believed that “There is only one fruit of this terrene life, a pious disposition and social acts”. This has a huge part in the purpose of life. Being a good neighbor is extremely important to who we become as we learn to look outside of ourselves. Mr. Rogers as previously mentioned thinks we need to mourn with those around us to walk with the people who are nearby. Being a good neighbor helps me to see how God treats his other children. It also allows me to ask God how he would like me to treat his children. Gandhi stated in his Autobiography that salvation could only come to those who put others before themselves in all scenarios. Putting others first requires a lot of effort in our day-to-day lives, but it is necessary because this life isn’t just about us.
