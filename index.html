Philosophy of Prayer

Attitude of prayer
Simone Weil mentions that prayer requires attention or focus. I agree with the idea that prayer requires a focus not because God won’t hear us but that we might not hear God if we don’t focus on what we are saying and feeling. Prayer for me is most effective when I’m alone and in a quiet place that is free from distractions. I try and treat it as a conversation, and I know that majority of the time I’ll have to do a lot of the talking. I share what is going on as if God knew nothing about my life. It helps me to freely discuss what is going on and what my worries are. I try to treat the prayer as if it was between me and a parent or someone who loves me. I find these prayers leave me feeling more peaceful.
What prayer can do
Prayer can lead to miracles. I know no other way of putting it. Those miracles can be diverse things including healing, blessings, help, and change. In the movie Shadowlands, C.S Lewis mentions that prayer might not change anything that is going on in our lives but through prayer, God can change us. On my mission, I had a rough time a couple of weeks into Covid. Nothing was going the way I hoped it would. I started to pray while writing in my journal about what was going on in my life. I expressed how angry, frustrated, and helpless I felt. At the end of that prayer, I had received some knowledge that I wasn’t expecting. God didn’t justify how I felt instead he showed me things that I could not see at the time. Nothing changed about the situation I was in, but I made it past day 24 of being confined to an apartment and made it to the 9-month mark. Nothing had astronomically changed about my situation it still sucked but I was changed which made something more possible.
Why I pray
C.S Lewis shares “He gave us small creatures the dignity of being able to contribute to the course of events in two different ways”. He explains that one of these ways is through prayer. I’ve seen this demonstrated when I’ve knelt on my knees during difficult moments and prayed for relief and been given the capacity to be cleaned of the worries of what I was struggling through. I pray because I know that I can learn about God. He teaches me as I communicate with him showing me things that I never would have been able to acknowledge without him. The main reason I pray might be more towards the pragmatist side. I pray because I’ve experienced it working in my own life and so I continue to do so.

Acknowledging Difficulties

In a Beautiful Day in the Neighborhood, Mr. Rogers explains that talking about something makes it a human problem. When something is a human problem, it becomes manageable. In my relationships, I’ve seen problems arise because something couldn’t be discussed. Someone was hurting and neither of us could help the other because we didn’t know how to talk about it and when we tried, we never managed to get to the heart of what was going on. I’ve found that I need to discuss challenging things when they arise. I feel morally obligated to do so because I’ve seen how much different life can be when we struggle through things together instead of being alone. In the Shadowlands, Joy discusses with C.S Lewis that her death they need to talk about. She explains that it will help both to enjoy what is going on know if they acknowledge the suffering that is to come. The scriptures teach us that we need to mourn with those that mourn and I think this principle starts with being able to talk about difficult topics.
